We predict that Slovak will produce the most errors, in terms of syntax, lexicality, and morphology across all parts of speech, then French, and then German. First of all, the target language is English. English and German are in the same language sub-family, and they are each more closely related to the romance language family -- which includes French -- than the Slavic famiy -- which includes Slovak. The Slavic languages contain extensive word level morphological markings (case systems) that are not present in either French or English. While German does have case marking and therefore syntactic ordering is flexible as in Slovak, which may not translate well into English automatically, note that Google Translate's processing is based on statistical analysis of huge information corporas. French and German have many more speakers than Slovak. In fact, Google Translate processes Slovak into Czech, a closely related but more somewhat more common language, before processing into English. Therefore, Google Translate has less data to base its statistical predictions on in processing Slovak to English texts.
