This project is an investigation of machine translation. Translating into English, we wanted to compare what kind of grammatical differences and errors Google Translate generates from the source languages German, Slovak, and English. We will attempt to answer the following research questions:
  • 1. Of Slovak, German, and French, which version will contain the most sentences that are in ungrammatical order after processing through Google Translate?
  • 2. In each of these languages, which parts of speech will be most affected by errors in translation?
  • 3. What features of these parts of speech were most affected, i.e., tense marking, case, etc.?
"Error" is a term used generically throughout our project to describe how we are attempting to capture ungrammaticality produced in machine translation. However, sometimes Google Translate may produce a technically grammatical structure that nevertheless differs substantially in meaning or form from the original French, German, or Slovak text. To describe what are tokenization scheme is truly capturing, we coined the term "divergences." The term "divergences" furthermore conveys the concept, although all three versions of our texts are ideally supposed to be English, the errors and mistranslations produced cause major discrepancies in how they are read and what they mean.
