Ungrammaticality in syntax was marked on a sentence level, where each sentence was tagged in XML and its attribute "tr" -- for "transposition" -- was determined "1," i.e., "true" for grammatical," or "0," i.e., "false" for ungrammatical. Errors or "divergences" were coded for by individual word in XML. These tags were called "difs." Dif attributes were part of speech and type as listed below.
  • Parts of speech
    • <det> Determiners: "the cat," "a mouse," "those tomatoes"
    • <n> Nouns: "He and Jane walked," "They went to the park,"
    • <v> Verbs: "Harry Potter flew on a broomstick," "He will go to Hogwarts"
    • <adj> Adjective: "Crookshanks was fluffy"
    • <adv> Adverb: "He was not there," "The three headed dog growled vicisouly
    • <pp> Preposition/prepositional phrase: "The cat sat in front of Privet Drive"
    • <pro> Pronoun: "She summoned Dumbledore," "Who found Neville's toad?"
    • <conj> "Ron laughed but Harry didn't," "Severus Snape, who was the Potions master"
  • Error Types
    • <ten> Determiners: "He will go last night"
    • <cas> Case: "He talked to she"
    • <num> Num: "We goes to Hogwarts," "The cat chase the mouse"
    • <gen> Gender: "Minerva talked to Dumbledore because she was the smartest professor at Hogwarts"
    • <mst> Adverb: "He ever called that late"
    • <moo> Mood: "He will go to the ball if he had been allowed,"
    • <asp> Aspect: "He spoken to him last week"
    • <per> Person: "I rides the broomstick"
    • <del> Deleted: "Ron talked to [the]" professor
    • <pos> Part of speech: "Malfoy laughed cruel"
As for our choice of text, Harry Potter was selected for several reasons. First of all, we are assuming that the corpora Google Translate works with are texts that are written in modern day speech. Using a classic, with outdated speech features, would thus negatively, possibly dramatically affect our results. Second of all, the Harry Potter books are universally known and widely translated. Moreover, their translations are actually relevant to readerships. As these books are read for pleasure, people would likely choose to read these books in their native languages, i.e., translated versions. The classics and literary writing are more popular in academic circles, where there is merit in reading these types of texts in their original language, i.e., not translated versions.
